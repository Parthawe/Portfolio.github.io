Office of Diversity Report & Data Visualization

Interactive IDBEA report for NYU Tisch School of the Arts

ClientOffice Of Diversity TSOA
ScopeUser Research, UI/UX, Prototyping
RoleWebsite Publishing Designer
Duration3 Months
Year2024

Translating complex data into engaging, accessible visuals that foster deeper community engagement.

Summary This project aimed to create an engaging, accessible, and interactive visualization for the 2024 IDBEA report, showcasing the Tisch School of the Arts’ progress in fostering inclusion, diversity, belonging, equity, and accessibility (IDBEA). Collaborating with Christina Monea from the Office of Diversity, my goal was to translate complex data into user-friendly visuals that reflect Tisch’s values and foster deeper community engagement.
Challenges • Data Precision — Visualizing complex data accurately while maintaining clarity and readability.
• Interactive Engagement — Developing dynamic components to encourage users to explore milestones and data points.
My Role • Data Visualization — Designing interactive timelines, charts, and graphs.
• User Experience — Ensuring a seamless and engaging user experience with interactive and accessible elements.
Tools & Techniques Figma User Research Identity and Website Design Prototyping Wireframes High-Fidelity Experience Management Design System

The IDBEA Report

Tisch’s Office of Diversity champions Inclusion, Diversity, Belonging, Equity, and Accessibility (IDBEA) across the school. The 2024 report documents a decade of progress — from early listening sessions and foundational learning to the establishment of a dedicated office and committee structures that ensure lasting institutional change.

01 — Process

Understanding The Scope

The first step involved deep-diving into the report’s key metrics, themes, and milestones. Three core focus areas emerged:

Learning & Engagement
Educational programs and community workshops driving awareness across Tisch.
Restorative Practices
Addressing IDBEA concerns and global events through structured case resolution.
Collaborative Efforts
School-wide programming that brings together faculty, staff, and students.
02 — Process

Design Conceptualization

An interactive timeline was designed to map IDBEA milestones spanning 2014–2024, alongside dynamic charts and graphs. Early ideation involved sticky notes, whiteboard sessions, and rapid sketching to explore visual directions.

03 — Process

Data Visualization

01
Tool Selection
D3.js and Tableau were chosen for their flexibility in rendering interactive, data-driven visuals.
02
Color-Coded Bar Charts
Engagement metrics visualized through a consistent color system tied to each IDBEA pillar.
03
Dynamic Pie Charts
Breakdowns of participation, demographics, and resource allocation rendered as interactive pie charts.
04
Interactive Tooltips
Hover and click-to-reveal tooltips providing context and detail on every data point.
04 — Process

Interactivity & User Engagement

Clickable Data Points
Each data point expands to reveal detailed information, case studies, and related milestones.
Real-Time Progress
Users can view Tisch’s IDBEA progress in real-time through animated transitions and live data feeds.
05 — Process

Collaboration & Feedback Integration

Over a 3-month process working with Christina Monea, the project involved multiple feedback sessions with faculty, students, and staff. This iterative cycle ensured the final product accurately reflected the community’s voice and the office’s mission.

06 — Process

Accessibility Compliance

WCAG 2.1 Level AA
Full compliance with established web accessibility guidelines.
Screen Reader Support
Text alternatives for all visual elements, ensuring screen reader compatibility.
High-Contrast Colors
Color schemes designed for maximum readability across all visual conditions.
Keyboard Navigation
Every interactive element fully navigable without a mouse.

Reflections

Key Learnings

Collaboration
Working closely with Christina Monea and the broader Tisch community was essential to producing an authentic and impactful report.

The Report

IDBEA 2024 Report

The final report traces a decade of IDBEA milestones — from the initial commitment in 2014 through listening sessions, foundational learning, the establishment of the Cabinet, the hiring of an Assistant Dean, and the expansion of the Office of Diversity — culminating in the formation of the IDBEA Committee in 2023.
