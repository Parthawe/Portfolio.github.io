Approach

I don’t believe in design that explains itself.
I believe in design that doesn’t need to.

Every product I build starts with the same question: what is the user afraid of right now? Not what features do they need — what fears do they carry into this interaction? A migrant worker sending money home. A developer building their first miniapp. A patient reading an AI diagnosis. The interface is the last thing I design. The emotional architecture comes first.

I work across the full stack of design: research, systems thinking, interaction design, visual craft, and prototyping. I build installations and typefaces alongside product work because understanding how people interact with physical space makes me a better designer of digital space.
